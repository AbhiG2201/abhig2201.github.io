Experience

Where I've worked

Graduate Research Assistant Aug 2025 – December 2025
NC State RARE Lab — Raleigh, North Carolina
  • Conducted 3D unsteady CFD simulations of a 3-ft rotor in ANSYS Fluent using a transient sliding mesh framework, resolving tip vortex shedding and wake contraction across RPM sweeps of 1,000 – 3,500 at angles of attack from -2 to 10 degrees to inform future morphing wing design.
  • Optimized mesh resolution and timestep selection to maintain numerical stability at constant blade tip speeds, applying boundary layer inflation and turbulence modeling best practices to preserve vortex structure through the downstream wake.
  • Quantified vorticity, induced velocity, pressure distributions, and tip vortex coherence across all mesh refinements, demonstrating that coarse meshes artificially overpredict thrust and underpredict torque due to insufficient resolution of blade-tip flow curvature.
Aerospace Engineering Intern Jun 2023 – Aug 2024
Jacobs — NASA Langley Research Center, Hampton, VA
  • Developed MATLAB scripts to automatically identify supersonic shock wave locations in the Unitary Plan Wind Tunnel, improving supersonic test accuracy and reducing model risk.
  • Designed and validated a high-pressure propulsion enclosure in SolidWorks, applying structural analysis and fabrication constraints to support expanded experimental testing capabilities.
  • Collaborated with Test Engineers to program and analyze large experimental aerodynamic datasets, resolving calibration and data quality issues to support model validation.
  • Developed a MATLAB GUI that automated Euler angle rotation and coordinate transformation calculations, reducing model setup and calibration time.
  • Documented multi-system flow infrastructure across wind tunnel facilities, supporting operational accuracy for high-pressure experimental test environments used in aerodynamic research and model validation
Engine Team Member Feb 2023 – Aug 2023
NC State Liquid Rocketry Lab — Raleigh, North Carolina
  • Evaluated liquid rocket engine nozzle designs in Siemens NX and implemented validated design strategies into team prototypes.
  • Executed coupled structural and thermal FEA in ANSYS Mechanical on liquid rocket engine components operating at 1,000 psi chamber pressure and 1,000 K, performing mesh convergence studies and post-processing stress and deformation results to validate designs.
Student Assistant Apr 2022 – Dec 2025
NC State Athletics — Raleigh, North Carolina
